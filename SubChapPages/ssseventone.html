Food for Help

It is greatly said that only needy knows the importance of a thing but a self-fulfilled person will not understand even the importance of a raw vegetable placed in front of him/her and for a poor and underprivileged person it can be their one time meal.

Society for Social Service organized a food drive at Jwala Heri earlier this year, where volunteers prepared delicious gourmet style sandwiches served to not so fortunate joey out there.

World has a number of problems with long term and irrevocable effects to the environment. Food shortage and wastage is one of the man created issues which can be solved easily, just by changing habits.

It is estimated that over 40% of food served every year is wasted or never consumed, which sums up to a vast amount of food accounting the large produces of the 21st century.

It is indeed ironical that even after huge produces we have enough food to throw in the dump instead of giving it to the ones in need.

Society of Social Service (SSS) in collaboration with BVPCSI concerned for such a trivial social issue organized a Food for Help drive, where students prepared fresh sandwiches to donate to the less fortunate at Jwala Heri, Paschim Vihar.
